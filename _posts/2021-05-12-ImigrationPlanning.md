Here I am going to sum up what I have done so far for learning Norwegian. I will introduce a few of practical resources which I am participating into. If you are also interested in Norwegian, this post could provide some useful tips for you.



The first resources I recommend is [Duolingo](https://www.duolingo.com/). I love this Application so much. The feeling while I am "playing" with this Application is incredible. There is no big vocabulary, no complicated grammar lecturing, just some daily-life sentences  practices for you at the beginning. The patten is similar with playing video games, which you will gain more badges, coins, and titles when you complete more and more challenges. There are several sections in each challenge, sentence re-structuring, vocabulary matching,  sometimes you have to type down the sentence pronounces in Norwegian. Also the user interface is so amazing that encourages you complete more and more challenge.



Beside Application, there are couples of Youtube Channels are beneficial in listening and knowing more about Norwegian culture. 

1. [Simple Norwegian](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCDisCabc2DDWSxP4nbvAoBA): the sparking point of this channel is interviews with Norwegian native speaker. Given some common and interesting questions, you will get the real response and answer from the Norwegian. This is a so abstracting me by knowing how to speak like native speaker, how to express your own ideas or feeling about common condition.
